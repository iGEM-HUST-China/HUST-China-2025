Squalene

Precursor to Skincare Miracle

Squalene is a naturally occurring lipid compound and the precursor to squalane, widely used in cosmetics.

Its unique molecular structure makes it a prized ingredient in skincare products.

Moisture-Locking Mechanism

Squalane forms a protective barrier on the skin's surface that prevents moisture loss.

This "lock-in rather than soak-in" property makes squalane an exceptional moisturizer.

Growing Market Demand

The global squalene market continues to expand with an annual growth rate exceeding 7.5%.

Used in premium cosmetics and pharmaceutical applications.

Squalene Sources

Traditionally extracted from deep-sea shark livers, placing pressure on marine ecosystems.

Plant-based sources have emerged as sustainable alternatives.

Waste Oil Pollution Crisis

Globally, over 30 million tons of used cooking oil are produced annually.

Causes severe water and soil pollution when improperly handled.

Squalene from Waste Oil

Our innovative project utilizes synthetic biology technology.

We convert waste oil into high-purity squalene using engineered microorganisms.

Shark Liver Source

Traditional but Unsustainable

For decades, squalene was primarily sourced from deep-sea shark livers.

This practice has led to the decline of shark populations and threatens marine ecosystems.

Approximately 3 million sharks are killed annually for squalene production.

Plant-Based Sources

Sustainable Alternatives

Plant sources like olive oil, amaranth, and rice bran offer sustainable alternatives.

These sources are renewable but face challenges with yield and production costs.

Plant-based squalene accounts for about 30% of the global market.

Biotechnological Production

The Future of Squalene

Modern biotechnology enables squalene production through fermentation.

Engineered microorganisms can convert renewable resources into high-purity squalene.

This approach offers scalability, sustainability, and purity advantages.
