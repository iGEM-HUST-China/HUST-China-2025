Our Innovation

We use synthetic biology to upgrade waste cooking oil (WCO) into cosmetic-grade squalane, protecting marine ecosystems while enabling low-carbon supply chains.

Squalane Benefits

Squalane closely resembles human sebum, allowing for quick absorption and strengthening the skin barrier. It's gentle, non-irritating, and suitable for sensitive skin and infant care products.

Market Potential

The global skincare and cosmetics industry's demand for green ingredients continues to grow, with sustainable and low-carbon supply chains becoming industry consensus and trend.

Sustainable Sources

Our approach uses engineered Yarrowia lipolytica to convert waste cooking oil into high-purity squalane through fermentation, providing a sustainable alternative to shark-derived squalane.

Waste Oil Crisis

China produces approximately 11-12 million tons of waste cooking oil annually. Improper handling causes serious food safety risks and environmental pollution.

Our Solution

Using engineered Yarrowia lipolytica as chassis, we process waste oil through lipase expression and flux redirection to produce high-quality squalane through fermentation.
